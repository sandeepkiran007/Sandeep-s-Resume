--Jim Rohn
Jogging with a friend is a great way to stay motivated and form a good workout habit. Exercising with someone around the same fitness level as you can spur each other on and make it more fun, increasing your chances of sticking to your exercise plan. It’s also a great way to meet new people

Running with another person is bound to start conversation and deepen connection. Talking and running is a great way to make those miles go by faster and even feel easier. Adding in a social component to exercise is likely to provide greater stress relief

Racing with a friend helps ease those starting line nerves. Plus you don’t have to be alone at the post-race party. Once you’ve crossed the finish line, it’s time to celebrate! And even if you don’t go to a race with a friend, you’re bound to know someone

Writing and journaling daily can have many benefits for the brain. Writing down thoughts and ideas that you have throughout the day can improve your memory as your brain is more likely to retain that information
